FAQ

Common Questions

Quick answers for users before they start a property document review.

Does PropertyTrace replace legal advice?

No. PropertyTrace is a document-review and risk-indicator support tool. It does not replace official land registry records, government verification, or professional legal advice.

What documents can I prepare before using it?

Common documents include deed or দলিল, khatian or land record, mutation record, tax receipt, previous ownership proof, inheritance document if applicable, and property measurement details.

Can scanned or handwritten papers be reviewed?

The front-end workflow is designed for scanned and digital documents. A production system would use OCR and manual review support for low-quality scans or handwritten content.

How is the risk score calculated?

The score is based on sample factors such as missing mutation records, name mismatch, unclear ownership chain, unpaid tax proof, and suspicious document gaps. It is a decision-support signal, not a legal decision.

Can I download a report?

The demo includes a report preview and sample download placeholder. In production, this can be connected to a generated PDF report.

Is document privacy important here?

Yes. Property documents can contain sensitive information. Users should avoid sending originals through demo forms and should use secure upload workflows in production.
